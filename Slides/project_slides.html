01 · Motivation

Why machine-learning weather forecasting?

  • Numerical weather prediction (NWP) is accurate but compute-heavy — hours on supercomputers per forecast.
  • Data-driven models (GraphCast, Pangu-Weather, FourCastNet) now match NWP skill at a fraction of the cost — seconds on a single GPU.
  • Southeast Asia is hazard-prone (typhoons, monsoon flooding) and densely populated, yet under-served by regional ML forecasting.
  • Goal: bring this capability to the region under realistic resource constraints.
02 · Objectives

Project objectives

  • Build a GraphCast-style GNN for the Southeast Asia domain at 0.25° resolution.
  • Train the full pipeline end-to-end on modest hardware (Kaggle, single/dual GPU).
  • Fuse reanalysis (ERA5) with satellite observations (NASA brightness temperature).
  • Evaluate rigorously against standard baselines and analyse which components matter (ablations).
  • Deliver a reproducible, scalable pipeline and a good insight into the regional weather forecasting capabilities.

04 · Data

Study region & data sources

  • Domain: 11°S–28.5°N, 92°E–142°E — ~159×201 grid at 0.25°.
  • ERA5 reanalysis, 6-hourly. Train 2020–2024 · Val 2025 · Test 2026.
  • Surface: 2 m temperature & dewpoint, MSLP, 10 m u/v wind, total precipitation.
  • Pressure levels (500 / 850 hPa): geopotential, specific humidity, u/v wind.
  • Static: land–sea mask, surface geopotential. Satellite: NASA brightness temperature (Tb).
05 · Pipeline

Data & processing pipeline

Module1 · ERA5 downloadModule2 · NASA satellite Module3 · preprocess → Zarr + normalization Module4 · trainModule5 · evaluate / demo
  • Preprocessing: regrid, z-score normalization (statistics from train years only), build residual labels, write per-year Zarr.
  • float16 + streaming Zarr to fit Kaggle storage limits.
  • DataLoader: on-the-fly Zarr reading, random cropping, batch assembly.
  • One-time memmap pre-decode at train time removes repeated decompression (see Engineering slide).
  • Training: PyTorch Lightning, mixed precision, multi-GPU DataParallel.
  • Evaluation: multi-start autoregressive rollout on the held-out test year; metrics include RMSE, ACC, regional error, power spectrum.
06 · Formulation

Problem formulation

  • Inputs: atmospheric state at t and t−1, plus satellite Tb and static fields.
  • Residual target: predict ΔX = X(t+6h) − X(t); output state = X(t) + ΔX.
  • Residual learning makes short-range prediction easier and more stable.
  • Autoregressive rollout: feed predictions back to forecast +6h … +72h.
  • Caveat: error compounds with lead time; evaluation focuses on the first 48h where skill is expected.

08 · Graph

Mesh & graph construction

  • Fine grid: 0.25° (~32k nodes) carries the high-resolution fields.
  • Coarse mesh: 2° (hundreds of nodes) carries long-range information.
  • Three edge sets: grid→mesh (encode), mesh→mesh (process), mesh→grid (decode).
  • Nodes carry latitude/longitude positional features; nearest-neighbour connectivity.
09 · Training

Training setup & loss

  • Loss: latitude-weighted RMSE (area weighting by cos φ).
  • Optimizer: AdamW · cosine LR schedule · gradient clipping · early stopping.
  • Mixed precision (AMP); residual prediction with 2-step input.
  • Trained on train years; model selected on the validation year; final scores on the held-out test year.

10 · Engineering

Training within Kaggle limits

  • Constraints: tens of GB of data, 30 GB GPU, limited RAM & session time.
  • Bottleneck found: Zarr chunking forced ~100× redundant decompression every epoch.
  • Fixes: one-time float16 memmap pre-decode · vectorized (batched) message passing · AMP · multi-GPU DataParallel.
  • Result: training time reduced from ~11h to ~6h per run, with zero OOMs and good scaling across 2 GPUs.

11 · Evaluation

Evaluation methodology

  • Protocol: multi-start autoregressive rollout — 30 start times × 12 steps (+72h) on the held-out test year.
  • Baselines: persistence · climatology · U-Net (best-performing CNN for weather forecasting).
  • Metrics: latitude-weighted RMSE · ACC (Anomaly Correlation Coefficient) · regional RMSE (land / sea / mountain) · power spectrum (blurring check).

RMSE

Magnitude of error vs ground truth, per lead time.

ACC

Pattern skill of anomalies; ≥ 0.6 = useful skill.

Spectrum

Detects over-smoothing of small-scale structure.

12 · Results

Skill vs lead time

  • The model beats persistence by a large margin (+37%).
  • Error grows smoothly with lead time, as expected.
  • Smooth, large-scale fields (temperature, geopotential, MSLP).
  • Small-scale, noisy fields (wind, precipitation) are harder to predict.
  • Overall, the model captures the main synoptic evolution well out to +48h, with room for improvement at longer horizons and smaller scales.
13 · Results

14 · Results

Regional error & spectral analysis

  • Regional RMSE pattern: sea < plain < land < mountain — physically consistent.
  • Complex terrain and the diurnal cycle drive the largest land/mountain errors.
  • Spectral analysis checks whether the model over-smooths small scales.
15 · Comparison

Baseline comparison

  • GraphCast GNN vs U-Net vs climatology vs persistence — all on the test set, identical rollout.
  • Honest finding: on a small regional domain at short lead, a U-Net baseline is competitive — CNNs have a strong inductive bias on regular grids.
  • The GNN's advantage is expected to grow with multi-scale meshes and longer horizons.

17 · Outlook

Limitations & future work

Limitations

• Learns ERA5-quality fields — inherits its 0.25° ceiling.
• Requires an initial analysis state to start.
• Regional, single-scale, 72h — a research proof-of-concept, not an operational system.

Future work (roadmap)

• Data: 10-year archive, 8 pressure levels, SST/TCWV, real DEM (GEBCO/ETOPO).
• Architecture: multi-scale icosahedral mesh, 10–30M parameters.
• Science: full ablations, multi-step fine-tuning, ACC/spectral/regional suite.
• Deployment: ONNX export, FastAPI + Streamlit dashboard.

Conclusion

A regional GraphCast, built on accessible hardware.

  • Trained a GraphCast-style GNN for Southeast Asia end-to-end on Kaggle.
  • Beats classical baselines; an honest, informative comparison against a U-Net.
  • Delivers a reusable pipeline, evaluation suite, and interactive demo.
